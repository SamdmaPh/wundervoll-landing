The philosophy behind Wundervoll

Built on the shoulders
of giants.

Seven researchers. Decades of science. Every design decision in Wundervoll traces directly back to evidence — not opinion, not intuition. Evidence.

Stephen Krashen
"Language is acquired, not learned. Stress blocks everything."
The Affective Filter Hypothesis
Anxiety raises the affective filter and blocks input from reaching the language acquisition device. A relaxed, emotionally safe learner acquires language. A stressed one does not.
In Wundervoll
Every lesson opens with a scene — not a goals list. The learner is emotionally in Berlin before they know they're learning grammar.
Paul Pimsleur
"The ear before the eye. Always. Audio is the mother tongue of language."
Graduated Interval Recall + Audio-First
Language is fundamentally a sound system. Reading is a secondary representation. Learning begins with the ear — exactly as in first-language acquisition.
In Wundervoll
Every phrase plays automatically before the learner sees text. The tap-to-reveal comes after the sound, never before.
Richard Schmidt
"You cannot acquire what you haven't noticed."
The Noticing Hypothesis
Conscious attention to form in input is necessary (though not sufficient) for acquisition. The learner must notice a feature before it can enter long-term memory.
In Wundervoll
Each lesson has exactly ONE noticing moment. The learner discovers it in the phrases they just heard — it is never explained, only revealed through guided attention.
Michel Thomas
"It is not the student's fault if they don't know. It is the teacher's fault."
Learner Responsibility Transfer
The teacher bears complete responsibility for creating conditions for learning. When a student fails, the teaching method has failed — not the student.
In Wundervoll
No red X. Wrong answers receive a warm explanation and the correct form. The app takes responsibility — not the learner.
Michael Lewis
"Language is not grammar plus vocabulary. It is chunks."
The Lexical Approach
Language consists of multi-word units (chunks) that should be learned as wholes — not assembled from grammar rules. "Entschuldigung!" is not a word. It is a social tool with a sound, a context, a feeling.
In Wundervoll
Phrases are presented as complete units with audio — heard, felt, used — before any grammar explanation appears.
Swain · Benny Lewis
"Speak from day one. Imperfect output beats perfect silence."
Output Hypothesis + Day-One Speaking
Merrill Swain demonstrated that pushed output — being required to produce language — accelerates acquisition beyond input alone. Benny Lewis proved this works with zero prior knowledge.
In Wundervoll
Every single lesson — including Lesson 1 — ends with a real conversation in German. No timer. No score. Just speak.
The Golden Rule of Wundervoll
Everything that helps the learner = English.
Everything the learner learns = Deutsch.
Every word of UI, instruction and feedback is in English for A1 learners. Every phrase, every example, every conversation — in German.
